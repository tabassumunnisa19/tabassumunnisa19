Loops of Parenthood

If you’ve ever wondered what parenting and Python have in common… it’s loops.

  • A baby cries → you feed them → they cry again → repeat until forever. That’s basically a while loop.
  • Toddlers asking “why?” a thousand times a day → that’s a for loop running with no break statement.
  • Parenting tip: unlike Python, you can’t just break out of these loops. (Trust me, I’ve tried saying continue and it didn’t help.)

But here’s the magic: every iteration teaches something new—patience, creativity, and how to sneak in chocolate without being caught. So whether it’s debugging code or bedtime stories, remember: life is just a loop, and sometimes the bugs are what make it fun.
